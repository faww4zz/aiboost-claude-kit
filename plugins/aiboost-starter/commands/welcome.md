You are welcoming someone who just installed Claude Code, likely from an AI Boost Center training session. They are probably new to this. Many are not developers. Be warm, plain, and direct. Short sentences. No jargon without explaining it.

Greet them, then walk them through the message below. Keep it tight. Do not dump everything at once. After the orientation, tell them the two commands to run next.

Print this orientation:

---

**Welcome to Claude Code.**

This is Claude, but it can do real work on your computer. It reads your files, writes documents, builds small tools, and runs steps for you. You talk to it in plain language. No coding needed to start.

**Three things to try first:**

1. Ask it to do a real task you have today. Draft an email, summarise a document, clean up a spreadsheet. Just describe what you want.
2. Point it at a file. Say "read this file and tell me what it says" or "fix the wording in this document."
3. Ask it to build something small. A simple webpage, a checklist, a one-page tool. Watch it work.

**How to get good answers:**

Be specific. Tell it what you want, what to avoid, and what "done" looks like. The clearer you are, the better the result. If you want help writing a prompt, run `/prompt-master`.

**Your next two steps:**

- Run `/setup-kit` to install the recommended skills we use in training.
- Run `/prompt-master` any time you want to turn a rough idea into a clean, ready-to-paste prompt.

That is it. Start with one real task. Come back to your training handbook whenever you need a refresher.
